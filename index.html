Interesting facts about Camel!

  • Scientific name : Camelus.
  • Camels can completely shut their
    nostrils during sandstorms.
  • It is known as "The Ship of Desert"
    because it can comfortably move across
    sand due to its padded feet.

Interesting facts about Chameleon!

  • Scientific name : Chamaeleonidae.
  • Almost half of all known species
    live in Madagascar.
  • They mainly change color in order
    to communicate or regulate body temperature.

Interesting facts about Flying-cicada!

  • Scientific name : Cicadoidea.
  • The loud whirring or buzzing sound
    is a chorus made by an all-male cicada.
    (Females make a much quieter sound by
    flicking their wings)
  • Most have red-colored eyes. But
    occasionally they have blue, white
    or grayish eyes.

Interesting facts about Panda!

  • Scientific name : Ailuropoda
    melanoleuca.
  • Giant pandas are good at climbing
    trees and can also swim.
  • An adult panda can eat 12-38 kilos of
    bamboo and can poop 28 kilos per day!

Interesting facts about Pigeon!

  • Scientific name : Columbidae.
  • They are intelligent enough to
    understand space and time.
  • They can find their way back to
    the nest from 1300 miles away.

Interesting facts about Lion!

  • Scientific name : Panthera leo.
  • A good gauge of a male's lion age
    is the darkness of his mane. The
    darker the mane, the older the lion.
  • A lion's roar can be heard from
    as far as 5 miles away.

Interesting facts about T-rex!

  • Scientific name : Tyrannosaurus rex.
  • Its scientific name comes from greek
    and latin words meaning 'Tyrant Lizard King'.
  • More than 20 aalmost-complete t rex skeletons
    have been found. The most perfect, nicknamed
    Sue, was unearthed in South Dakota, USA,
    20 years ago, and was sold to a museum in
    Chicago for around 5 million pounds in 1997.

Interesting facts about Whale!

  • Scientific name : Cetacea.
  • Whales are the largest animals to
    have ever lived - even larger
    than any dinosaur!
  • Gray whales make one of the longest
    annual migrations of any mammals.
    They travel about 10,000 miles
    round trip!
